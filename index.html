Welcome!

Hello, I’m Abdollah Eydi! I'm a passionate mechatronics engineer Interested in robotics, machine learning, deep learning, control theory and path planing. I received my M.Sc. from the Sharif University of Technology. There, I was a member of the Social and Cognitive Robotics Lab, lead by Prof. Ali Meghdari.
I was involved in developing a social assistance robot called Arash; that's a robot to help children with cancer. I collaborated on this project to develop a ROS-based software framework for working with the Arash robot and developing robot electronic boards. Besides, as part of my master project, I developed a sound source localization system to improve the robot's interaction with humans.
I have also collaborated on the Rasa robot project as an electronic board designer.
My other experience is working in a startup about cloud service and developing a framework to build android and ios apps.

A Social Robot to Assist Children with Cancer in Hospitals

A mobile social robot named “Arash” has been designed for educational and therapeutic interventions for children with cancer in a hospital environment. Many children are hospitalized for different diseases, among these children with cancer experience special condi-tions and needs due to complex and painful surgeries. In particular, they suffer from physical pain caused by both cancer and its treatments process. Moreover, cancer treatment causes emotional distress which can reduce the efficiency of the treatments. Using social robots to interact with children in a hospital environment could decrease these children’s distress, thereby improving the efficiency of their treatment.

“Arash” is a mobile social robot designed and developed for pediatric cancer interventions for children aged from 4 to 12 years old in order to accommodate their needs and conditions (pre-and-post surgery) in a hospital environment. The design of the robot’s appearance and look were based on evaluation of several design alternatives identified in surveys taken by children with chronic diseases at three hospitals in Tehran.

  • Date: January 2017
  • Client: Threads
